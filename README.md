# 100 Hires Research Project

This repository documents my research on building a practical cold outreach pipeline for B2B SaaS.

I focused on people who are still active, still publishing, and still sharing what actually works in the field.  
The goal was not to collect generic sales advice, but to gather execution-level insights I can apply directly to outreach campaigns.

## What I Collected

- A curated list of 10 outbound experts in `research/sources.md`
- YouTube transcript notes in `research/youtube-transcripts/`
- Latest public LinkedIn post summaries in `research/linkedin-posts/`
- A short tool snapshot in `research/other/tools.md`
- My working playbook ideas in `research/other/ideas.md`

## Why I Chose These Experts

I selected experts who cover different parts of the same outbound system:

- Cold email structure and personalization (Alex Berman, Guillaume Moubeche, Nick Abraham)
- Sales messaging psychology and objection handling (Josh Braun, Jeremy Miner)
- Team-level outbound execution and revenue systems (Morgan J Ingram, Kyle Coleman, Becc Holland, Florin Tatulea)
- Deliverability and email performance optimization (Will Allred)

Most importantly, these are practitioners who regularly share recent, tactical content.  
That makes the research more useful for testing real campaigns, not just learning theory.

## Key Patterns From the Research

- Strategy consistently matters more than tools.
- Personalization works best when it is specific and tied to a real business problem.
- Multi-channel outreach (especially LinkedIn + email) helps warm prospects before direct asks.
- Fast testing cycles are critical: test multiple message variants and cut weak performers quickly.

## Tools Mentioned Across Sources

- Cold email: Lemlist, Smartlead, Instantly
- LinkedIn: Sales Navigator
- Email optimization: Lavender

## How I Plan to Use This

I will use this research as a baseline playbook to:

1. Build campaign messaging frameworks faster
2. Run structured outreach experiments
3. Improve reply quality and conversion over time

The output here is meant to stay practical: learn from proven operators, test quickly, keep what works.
